Happy Family

Happy families have strong family bonds. Parents have to be responsible for strengthening and protecting these bonds. It doesn’t happen naturally in our hectic day-to-day lives. You can create this firm foundation from our 10 useful tips that we also incorporate into our day-to-day life that will strengthen your family’s relationship:

10 Useful Tips To What Makes A Happy Family

  1. Create a mission statement

    It may seem a little too business-like, but it works. A family mission statement can remind every family member about core values of what you love most about each other. Place your mission statement in a predominant place in your home. Read it and talk about it more often.

  2. The power of prayer

    The power of prayer in a family is the very foundation that keeps a family unit together. “A family that prays together stays together”. This concept is so true, the Almighty has it's foundation that may be shaken at times, but it will withstand all that comes against them.

  3. Eat meals together

    Studies have shown that eating meals together helps reinforce communication. Choose a few nights during the week when you expect everyone to gather around the dinner table. Don't allow phones or other electronics either. Just eat a meal and have a conversation together.

  4. Schedule family time

    Take a look at everyone’s schedule. Try to make a regular night, maybe once a week, when the entire family gets together for a fun activity. Keeping it on a regular schedule, everyone will know that they need to keep that night clear for family times.

  5. Get involve in your children's interests

    When your family is working as a team, everyone feels supported and able to contribute. It helps to have clear expectations, limits and boundaries. Ask where you can help, it will show your children you care about what they are interested in.

  6. Encourage support

    Family support is important and you can build this bond that will last your kids a lifetime, even when they're your age and after you're gone. Encourage everyone to learn about things that are important to everyone else and to support each other through good and bad times.

  7. Have family meetings

    Family meetings are a good time for everyone to check in with each other, grievances, or discuss future plans. These can be scheduled events or you can make them impromptu and allow any member of the family to call a meeting if they feel the need.

  8. Do chores as a family

    Make cleaning your home or caring for the yard a responsibility for the whole family. Create a list of chores and have everyone involved. Set up a time when everyone can tackle their chores at the same time. Give them a set time to have their chores completed.

  9. Fight faster

    The most important things said in an argument are usually said in the first 3 minutes. The aim of any argument with your partner should be about reaching a resolution. From there, it's just a bunch of rephrasing, but louder. At the end don't forget to say sorry!

  10. Volunteer

    Giving your time to make someone else’s life better is always a powerful learning experience. Learning important life lessons together will strengthen the relationship you have with your children. This will demonstrate a positive experience upon your children to acknowledge.
