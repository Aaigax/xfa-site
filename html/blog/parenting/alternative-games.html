Alternatives To Video Games

Most children love video games, and the fascination with electronic entertainments such as game consoles, cell phones, and handheld devices is all part of our culture’s influence on technology. We believe that video games can teach certain skills or be educational to many children, but children spend too many hours with a controller in their hand primarily for pure entertainment, not educational. This will of course become an obsession that slowly starts to replace other activities and tear their life and their families apart.

Video games have been linked to a list of problems but there is a lot you can do to prevent this from occuring. In balance, “Limit Are The Keys” and then decide on your approach. Reassure them first and discuss alternatives activities to video games that will nourish your child’s developing mind to be even healthier as a result.

    What Have You Told Yourself?

  • He’s having fun, and it’s a cheap and easy babysitter
  • The game is making him tech savvy-he will need that skill in his future job
  • He is going to be a game designer
  • He is safe when he is on his game, for at least I know where he is
  • He is obsessed now, but I’m sure he will outgrow it
  • It’s just a harmless game
  • Dad plays with him, and it’s their only bonding time

    Signs To Watch Out For

  • Significant weight gain or weight loss
  • Mood changes and laziness
  • Avoiding friends and family members
  • Lying about the time spent playing video games
  • Skipping meals
  • Poor work performance
  • Poor academic performance

8 Useful Alternative Activities To Video Games

  1. Keep computers and consoles out of a child's bedroom.

    As far as possible keep the console and computer visiable at all times. This is perhaps the very first step parents should take to monitor online activity if computers and consoles are in open spaces.

  2. Assign video game time limit

    Kids need limits and boundaries to know what is normal and healthy for them. Limiting the amount of time that your children spend sitting in front of a television, playing video games and surfing the Internet is important, especially in today's world of technology. Request the child to help and cooperate with you in this matter.

  3. Show the child that you care

    Start the discussion by telling your child that you love him/her and that you are concerned about her/his happiness and well-being. Discuss the harms of addictive video gaming. Tell them specifically about the dangers it causes as fatigue, laziness, poor grades at school, lack of self-esteem etc. Discuss alternative activities to video games.

  4. Consistency is a critical element for effective parenting

    Parental words and promises must always match parental actions. If you set a daily limit, it must be enforced. If you let your child get away with breaking rules without consequences, they will not take you seriously. Make sure to stick to your word if your child breaks the rules.

  5. Read, Read, And Read More

    Get your kids excited about reading, and you've earned about six gold stars. Stories in books can have an advantage because they get use to their own imagination which provides more satisfying and rich narrative than a lot of games do. Importantly, reading for pleasure helps kids develop academically.

  6. Encourage your child to find other activities

    Video games are only one way that children can entertain themselves. There are a lot of things they can do instead. Encourage your child to pursue other interests, and if they can't think of anything, suggest a few for them. Learn to play a musical instrument, learn a new language or take on some other big challenge. Of course, as a parent your participation is required to help finance the project and help see it to completion.

  7. Go outdoors to appreciate nature

    Get out of your house and get out of the concrete jungle to take time to appreciate the natural world. The best remedy for those who are afraid, lonely or unhappy is to go outside. I firmly believe that nature brings solace in all trouble and you'll also feel refreshed.

  8. Encourage active hobbies

    Encourage active hobbies instead. These can range from team sports, to bike riding, hiking - anything that gets your child outside and moving.This can also foster a love of exercise, something that will be a valuable interest to have throughout their lives.

It’s okay to have mindless fun once in a while, video games can be a fun way to escape. But a teen who spends much of their free time in front of a computer or video game console is missing out on the countless opportunities waiting just outside the front door. Like anything else, too much of something can be too much. Make sure that you don’t rely on video games for babysitting or because “there’s nothing to do”. It is easy to get into the habit without realising it but should invest much of their precious time into things that bring value to their lives.
